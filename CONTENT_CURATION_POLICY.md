# PASTE Content Curation Policy

This policy defines how symbols are organized and surfaced in a privacy-first, utility-first symbol map.

## Product-direction alignment

- Desktop-first, mobile-usable UX
- Search-first workflow
- Instant copy on click/tap
- Neutral featured defaults
- Sensitive/controversial symbols remain accessible but are not featured by default
- No backend moderation service in v1 (policy is applied in dataset/editorial review)

## 1) Default featured content

Featured/default surfaces should favor broad, everyday utility:
- punctuation and quotation marks
- math and currency symbols
- arrows, checks/crosses, stars, shapes
- practical emoji commonly used in general communication

Do **not** feature by default:
- political symbols
- religious devotional symbols
- extremist or hate-associated symbols
- conflict/military insignia strongly tied to trauma

## 2) Access policy for sensitive/controversial symbols

Sensitive symbols are not hidden or removed only due to sensitivity.
They should remain available through:
- direct search,
- relevant categories,
- explicit curated packs (if justified).

They should not be promoted in homepage/default featured modules.

## 3) Editorial language rules

When contextual notes are needed:
- keep language factual and concise,
- avoid advocacy,
- prefer “associated with” phrasing,
- include region/time context when interpretation varies.

## 4) Category and taxonomy rules

- Use neutral, descriptive categories.
- Keep primary categories stable over time.
- Allow multi-context interpretation via tags/notes rather than forced single-meaning framing.
- Separate canonical Unicode identity from social context metadata.

## 5) Curated packs policy

Curated packs are allowed and encouraged for practical entry points.

Requirements:
- clear user-facing purpose,
- documented inclusion logic,
- neutral naming,
- respect default-featured neutrality constraints.

## 6) Knowledge source preference

For factual context and references, prefer:
1. Unicode
2. CLDR
3. Wikidata
4. DBpedia
5. Other neutral open references

Avoid Wikipedia-first linking behavior in default knowledge design.
